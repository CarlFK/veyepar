Ideally that page will let you do all you need and you don't have to read this next part.

It also contains a link the original interface that lets you do all sorts of things, like trim cruft from the start and end before the end. That last part means 'cut the recording so the part that isn't the talk isn't included.'

If a file contains cruft, like 5 minutes of people coming into the room and finding a seat, you should remove that. Same with the end. Introductions are nice, if they are nice. The encoder doesn't care if you cut stuff off or not, but the people who view the video will. Use your judgment.

You can trim from the start and end by entering the time in the Start and End boxes. The time you enter is the time you see on the player. Hit Play, when you are at the point you want the talk to start, hit Pause. look at the time ont the player, enter that in the Start box. Run the slider to the end and Play. If you talk ends long before the end of the file, hit Pause at the end of your talk, enter the time you see on the player in the End box.
